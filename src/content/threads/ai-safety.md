I keep coming back to AI safety because it sits at an uncomfortable
intersection: the most important technical problem of the decade, and one
where the social, philosophical, and political dimensions matter as much as
the math. It's a thread you can't really pull on from one direction.

What pulls me specifically is the question of how systems that are smarter
than us at narrow things — and increasingly broader things — get aligned
with what we actually want, when we ourselves don't agree on what we want.
The problem touches engineering, governance, epistemics, and the older
question of how humans cohere around shared meaning at all.

I'm not coming in as a researcher. I'm coming in as someone who reads, who
builds, who notices. The thread is here because the noticing has been
happening long enough that it's earned a section.
