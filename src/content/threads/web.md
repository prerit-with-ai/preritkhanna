The web is the medium I keep returning to because it's the cheapest form of
seriousness. A static page in 2026 still loads in any browser written in any
of the last twenty years. URLs outlive companies. Markdown outlives editors.
A site, if you build it right, becomes a notebook that compounds.

I'm pulled to the web less for the *web* and more for what it lets me hold
together: writing, projects, sketches, conversations, the slow accretion of
thinking across years. The technology is incidental; the durability is the
point.

The version I want to be good at is the slow version — the kind that
respects the reader's time, has zero chrome, loads instantly, says what it
means. Less Medium. More Gwern. Warmer than Gwern. The work is figuring out
what *warmer than Gwern* means in practice.
