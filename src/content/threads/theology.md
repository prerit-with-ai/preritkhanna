Theology is on the list because the deepest questions a person asks are
eventually theological, even when the language is borrowed from somewhere
else. *What is a good life. What do I owe to others. What survives me.* You
can run at these questions through philosophy, through psychology, through
literature — and you'll keep ending up at the same wall.

I want to take the questions seriously without taking any single answer
prematurely. The interesting work is at the seam between traditions —
between religious and secular framings, between Eastern and Western
inheritances, between what people *say* they believe and what their lives
actually move toward.

This thread will be slow. It earns its place by sitting next to the others
honestly: the engineering thread, the AI thread, the writing thread. None
of them get to dismiss the others.
