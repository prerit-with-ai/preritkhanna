# VISION.md

The north star for preritkhanna.com. Read this first. Everything else flows from here.

---

## What this is

preritkhanna.com is a personal site, but not the kind that pretends to be an essay archive or a portfolio. It's a notebook with many modes — a place for the building, the thinking, the watching, the wanting, the reading, the noticing — all by one person who refuses to be summarized as one thing.

Loosely framed as a Da Vinci-style notebook with anime-coded earnestness and a Naruto/Luffy-shaped relationship to ambition. There is no overarching brand pasted across the top. _Fool's Errand_ is one section. _The Guild_ is another. The site itself is just the name.

---

## The five jobs

Most personal sites do one or two of these. This one is designed to hold all five at once.

1. **Mirror.** Reflects who I actually am, in my full multiplicity, to myself and to readers. Not a curated highlight reel.
2. **Workshop.** Where things get made — projects, writing, sketches, foundations, fragments. Visible-in-progress.
3. **Forcing function.** Public visibility creates pressure to finish what I start. Incomplete is visible. Abandoned is named, with reasons.
4. **Coordination surface.** Where the guild assembles. Cool people who want to build cool things together find each other here.
5. **Long-arc story.** A continuous narrative across years. Future me, and future strangers, can read the arc — not just the latest entry.

These pull in different directions. _Mirror_ wants honesty. _Workshop_ wants in-progress mess. _Forcing function_ wants visible incompleteness. _Coordination_ wants clarity. _Story_ wants continuity. Good design holds all five.

## Orientation

The values that shape every design and content decision.

### Build first; gain depth in time

Don't pre-emptively read three books before starting a project — that's procrastination disguised as preparation. Start. The work tells me what I need to learn. Depth accumulates _as a consequence_ of building, not a prerequisite. Over years, this should compound: real depth in several domains, gained the right way.

### Foundations, then everything

I love starting from first principles for any new domain. The site reflects this. `/foundations` is a real mode where I write my own first-principles distillations of fields I'm learning — not summaries of others' work, but my framing of a domain after I've sat with it. Few personal sites have this. The depth proves the polymathy isn't tourism.

### Multi, not single

I want to be known as many things at once: tech, comedy, science, philosophy, theology, history, geopolitics, arts — and more domains as they pull at me. The site's shape — fifteen modes side by side, threads cross-cutting — says this without saying it. No "primary identity" copy anywhere. No "I'm a [X] who also does [Y, Z]." I'm trying to be many things at once, and the site shows the work.

### Earnest, unembarrassed, allergic to performance

I'd rather _be_ the thing than _look like_ the thing. The site has no follower counts, no "subscribe" pop-ups, no LinkedIn voice, no "Day 47 of building in public" framing, no curated vulnerability optimized for engagement.

Self-deprecating humor stays — it's craft, not defense. Naruto is goofy as hell. Luffy is a comic character. Da Vinci was famously vain and weird. Comedy that's actually funny isn't performance.

What goes is the _defensive_ self-deprecation: hedging the ambition before stating it, pre-lowering expectations so no one else has to. Naruto says he'll be Hokage flatly. Luffy says he'll be King of the Pirates without "lol probably not but." That's the energy.

**Be funny about everything except the ambition. State the ambition straight.**

### Always recruiting

The site is a guild hall. _If you're cool and we could make something together, this is where you find me._ The Guild section makes this explicit. The whole site implies it.

### Honest about incomplete and abandoned

The forcing function only works if the unfinished is visible. `/garden` exists for exactly this. Abandoned errands keep their abandonment notes — a one-paragraph "why I let it go." Naruto and Luffy don't hide their losses; neither does the site.

### Simple wherever possible — without trading off

No CMS, no over-engineering, no premature abstractions, no dependencies for convenience. But no shortcuts on what actually matters: the spine (markdown + Zod), URL stability, schema honesty, typography, writing.

The minimalism is foundation, not goal. Nothing should distract from the work.

## Influences

Three figures define the _shape_ of the ambition:

- **Naruto** — relentless, earnest, hidden depth, the worker who isn't a prodigy but never gives up. _"I will be Hokage."_ Said flatly, often, while being clowned for it.
- **Luffy** — childlike obsession with adventure, refuses to compromise on what he wants, assembles his crew by helping them with their quests first. _"I'll be King of the Pirates."_ No hedge.
- **Leonardo da Vinci** — polymath, fragments, sketchbooks, interconnected curiosity across domains. The Mona Lisa took 16 years. Slowness is a value.

What they share: **unembarrassed wanting.** None of them perform coolness. None of them couch their ambition. They're all in motion, all earnest, all outwardly trying.

Five additional figures whose work models pieces of this:

- **Richard Feynman** — playful polymath, foundations-from-scratch, willing to look stupid in pursuit of understanding.
- **Anthony Bourdain** — earned breadth across food, writing, travel, culture; comedy and seriousness in the same sentence.
- **Maggie Appleton** — the canonical polymath digital garden as form. Direct study material for what this site can be.
- **Patrick Collison** — founder-as-intellectual; public reading lists; cross-domain curiosity made habit.
- **Brian Eno** — generative across forms; _A Year With Swollen Appendices_ as the model of a polymath's working diary.

This list updates over years. Influences earn their place by actually shaping the work.

### Cross-cutting views

Auto-generated, not separately authored. Each is one route that aggregates from existing data:

- `/garden` — everything currently incomplete (drafts, paused errands, queued bucketlist items). The forcing function lives here.
- `/trying` — everything currently active. The captain's quest across modes.
- `/crossings` — essays tagged `crossings` (cross-domain noticings, the synthesis fragments).
- `/post-mortems` — essays tagged `post-mortem` (what I learned from abandoned errands).
- `/months/[YYYY-MM]` — monthly reflection essay.
- `/years/[YYYY]` — annual reflection essay.
- `/tags/[name]` — auto-cross-mode tag pages.
- `/threads/[name]` — curated thread pages (these are hand-written, not pure-aggregate).

### What threads do that nothing else does

Most polymath sites publish across domains and let tags carry the cross-cutting load. Tags are auto-generated and undifferentiated. **Threads are _narrated_ domains.**

Each thread is a domain or sustained interest I've explicitly chosen as central. Not every tag becomes a thread. A thread has:

- A name (Robotics, Quantum, Theology, Philosophy, Psychology, AI Safety, Language, History of Science, ...).
- A 2–5 paragraph "why this thread pulls me" — first person, present tense, in voice.
- An auto-aggregated index: errands, essays, foundations writeups, library books, radar items, quotes, bucketlist items, all tagged with this thread.
- Links to closely-related threads (Theology connects to Philosophy connects to Psychology).

Every entry on the site shows its threads at the top. So when a stranger reads any single piece, they immediately see the polymath context. **Threads make breadth navigable, in voice.** This is the differentiator.

## What this site is _not_

- **Not a portfolio.** No "Skills," no "Experience," no "Education" sections.
- **Not a blog.** Essays are one mode of fifteen. Chronological reading is not the optimization.
- **Not a CMS-backed product.** Content is markdown, in git, forever. No databases, no Notion, no admin panels.
- **Not a personal-brand vehicle.** No newsletter funnel, no content schedule, no growth metrics.
- **Not optimized for skim.** It rewards reading. Performant and clean — but the depth is on the page, not in summaries.
- **Not a museum.** It's a workshop. The unfinished is visible. The abandoned is named.

## Aesthetic

Minimal in chrome, dense in thought.

Serif body. Off-white background. Ink-dark text. One accent color used rarely. Zero icons, animations, illustrations — _except_ hand-drawn sketches when content warrants them. System fonts (no web font loading; performance is a floor).

Reference points: **gwern.net** for seriousness, **darioamodei.com** for restraint, **maggieappleton.com** for warmth, **stripe.press** book pages for typography. Closer to Maggie than to Gwern; warmer than Dario; more textured than Sam Altman.

Voice modulates by mode. Dispatches punchier. Foundations denser. Quotes quieter. Errands closest to how I actually talk. Essays most polished. Now fragmentary and intimate.

## Discipline

The design only works under discipline. Naming the failure modes so they stay named:

1. **The home page lists 8–10 surfaces, not 15.** The most-active. Others reachable through deeper navigation.
2. **Empty surfaces don't exist.** A path is created the day it gets its first real entry.
3. **Inactive surfaces get retired.** If a surface goes 12 months untouched, it's archived to a footer link with a note. No pretending.
4. **Depth somewhere is the proof.** A polymath site without real depth in any single mode reads as dilettante. Across years, real depth must accumulate in several places. `/foundations` is where this is most visible.
5. **Build the site, then live in the site.** Building can become an excuse to not write into it. After v1 ships, no site work until at least three new entries are added across the existing modes. Eat the dogfood.

---

## URLs are forever

Once a piece is published, its URL never changes. Slugs are picked with this in mind. Renames keep old URLs working via redirects. Sites that survive decades respect their URLs; sites that don't, rot.

---

## The reader's experience

Most polymath sites are intimidating to enter. The home page has a small "If you're new" section: 3–5 curated entries that, in sequence, give a stranger a real picture. Updates quarterly as the body grows. Lowers the barrier to entry without sacrificing depth.

Every entry shows its threads at the top. So a reader following one piece can see the surrounding intellectual context immediately and follow the thread to the rest.

The librarian (v3+) eventually replaces site-wide search by retrieving real passages from real essays/errands/notes and quoting them back, with attribution. Not impersonation. Honest retrieval.
